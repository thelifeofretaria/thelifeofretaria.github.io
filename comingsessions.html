The next session will be about eco-physiology, with:

Haruka Takagi
Eco-physiological study on planktonic foraminifera: What photophysiological measurements can tell us?
Planktonic foraminifera are a proliferated group among Rhizaria in pelagic environments. One of the key ecological traits that has enabled them to exploit a niche in warm oligotrophic environments is photosymbiosis —endosymbiosis with microalgae— which is a mutually beneficial nutritional relationship. In my research, I have been focusing on this ecology through assessing the photophysiology of symbionts in situ in planktonic foraminifera. Active chlorophyll fluorometry measurements can acquire parameters of photosynthetic performance of symbionts in a non-invasive way, therefore it can be used in combination with culturing studies in the lab, and enables instant measurements on board during sampling cruises for unculturable species. In this seminar, I will first introduce the basics of photosymbiosis in planktonic foraminifera, and then the application of photophysiological measurements in photosymbiosis research, including our recent experimental study on photosymbiotic responses to ocean warming. I will also present its application on benthic foraminifera and other Rhizaria.
